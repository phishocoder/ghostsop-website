# GhostSOP.com Audit

## Goal of the Audit

Document what the live site currently says, how it is structured, where the messaging misses the mark, and why the rebuild needs to reposition GhostSOP as a one-time lead handling system install instead of a broad AI or managed service offer.

## Current Site Structure

1. Hero
   - Headline: `Stop Losing Leads. Start Getting Time Back.`
   - Subcopy: promises an intake and follow-up system built in `48 hours`
   - Pricing callout starts at `$500`
   - CTAs: `Apply for a Sprint` and `Download the free playbook`
2. Feature strip
   - `Qualified Leads (No Back-and-Forth)`
   - `No Website Needed`
   - `Delivered in 48 Hours`
3. How it works
   - Three-step install process
4. Before/after comparison
   - `WITHOUT GHOSTSOP` vs `WITH GHOSTSOP`
5. Pricing section
   - `Basic`
   - `Premium`
   - `Always-On Front Desk (Managed)`
   - `Monthly Oversight`
6. Social proof section
   - Two unattributed testimonial snippets
7. Footer/contact
   - Directs people to Instagram or email

## Current Copy Inventory

### Messaging themes in use now

- `GhostSOP helps small business owners convert more leads with smart AI-powered intake tools`
- `We build your intake + follow-up system in 48 hours`
- `Starting at $500`
- `No hype. Just a calmer, more consistent way to run your front door.`
- `No Website Needed`
- `Optional routing + monitoring when you want it managed`
- `Monthly Oversight Starting at $49/month`

### Offer framing in use now

- Mixes one-time setup with managed service language
- Mixes operational setup with pricing-led sales copy
- Uses `AI-powered` language in metadata and positioning
- Introduces a managed monthly oversight offer that conflicts with the current blueprint

## CTA Placement and Clarity

### What exists

- Primary hero CTA: `Apply for a Sprint`
- Secondary hero CTA: `Download the free playbook`
- Footer CTA: `Shoot us a DM on Instagram` or email directly

### Problems

- CTAs split attention between booking, downloading, and social outreach
- No unified on-page lead capture form
- No clear "this is for fixing intake" CTA path
- `Apply for a Sprint` is vague and does not describe the result

## Messaging Weaknesses

### Positioning drift

- The live site leads with a vague promise about saving time and uses `AI-powered` metadata instead of the real core problem: lost leads due to slow response and broken intake.
- The current page sounds closer to a lightweight automation service or assistant setup than a lead handling infrastructure company.

### Offer confusion

- Pricing tiers and monthly oversight suggest GhostSOP is a productized service with ongoing management.
- `Always-On Front Desk (Managed)` directly conflicts with the source-of-truth offer: a one-time system install.
- `No website needed` and `Delivered in 48 hours` are useful operational notes, but they dominate the offer more than the actual outcome.

### Proof and trust issues

- The site uses testimonial-style quotes with no names, companies, or verifiable context.
- The offer claims strong operational outcomes without concrete explanation of what gets installed.
- Managed services and monthly support are introduced without enough explanation, which weakens trust.

### ICP targeting issues

- The page is written for very broad "small business owners" instead of appointment-based or inquiry-driven service businesses.
- It does not clearly say GhostSOP is for businesses that already get leads but lose them in the handoff.

## What Is Working

- The live site already recognizes the core operational pain: leads sit unread, follow-up is inconsistent, and manual intake causes chaos.
- The before/after framing is directionally strong and should be preserved in a sharper, more visual form.
- `No hype` and operational language point in the right tone direction.

## What to Preserve

- The core problem area: lost leads after the inquiry arrives
- The before/after framing
- The idea of intake + follow-up + routing as one system
- The anti-hype tone when it is tied to concrete deliverables

## What to Remove

- `AI-powered` as the lead message
- Pricing-led hero language
- `Apply for a Sprint`
- `Download the free playbook`
- Monthly oversight / managed service language
- Any implication that GhostSOP is a marketing agency, ongoing agency partner, or generic assistant product
- Unverified testimonial snippets

## Major Rebuild Decisions and Rationale

1. Lead with missed leads, not AI
   - Rationale: the Master Blueprint is explicit that GhostSOP should not lead with AI or general automation language.
2. Reframe GhostSOP as a one-time install
   - Rationale: the current site sells mixed setup and managed plans, which creates offer confusion and contradicts the blueprint.
3. Replace pricing tiers with concrete deliverables
   - Rationale: this builds trust without inventing proof and keeps attention on what gets installed.
4. Route every CTA to a single on-page form
   - Rationale: the current CTAs fragment attention across multiple actions and channels.
5. Clarify who the offer is for and not for
   - Rationale: the current site is too broad and attracts mismatched buyers.
6. Use a darker, more infrastructure-oriented design language
   - Rationale: the current page feels like a Carrd-style service page. The rebuild should feel like a credible operations product site for service businesses.

## Rebuild Summary

The current site identifies the right problem but packages it in the wrong offer. The rebuild should keep the operational pain and before/after logic, then replace the rest with a tighter message: GhostSOP fixes lead handling, not lead generation, through a one-time system install for service businesses that already get inquiries.
